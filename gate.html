GATE ECE Subject-wise Syllabus

MA

Engineering Mathematics

Linear algebra, calculus, differential equations, complex variables, and probability

↓
Linear Algebra: Vector algebra, systems of linear equations, eigenvalues and eigenvectors.
Calculus: Functions of single variable, limit, continuity, differentiability, mean value theorems, evaluation of definite and improper integrals, partial derivatives, total derivative, maxima and minima.
Differential Equations: First order equations (linear and non-linear), higher order linear differential equations with constant coefficients.
Complex Variables: Cauchy-Riemann equations, Cauchy's integral theorem and formula, Taylor's and Laurent's series.
Probability and Statistics: Mean, median, mode, standard deviation, random variables, Poisson, normal and binomial distributions, correlation and regression analysis.
NS

Networks, Signals & Systems

Circuit analysis theorems, linear time-invariant systems, and transform methods

↓
Network Solution Methods: Nodal and mesh analysis, network theorems (Thevenin, Norton, Superposition, Maximum Power Transfer).
LTI Systems: Continuous-time and discrete-time LTI systems, response, convolution, stability, causality.
Transforms: Fourier series, Fourier transform, Laplace transform, Z-transform, region of convergence (ROC).
Sampling Theorem: Nyquist rate, aliasing, signal reconstruction.
ED

Electronic Devices (EDC)

Carrier transport, semiconductor junctions, BJTs, MOSFETs, and optoelectronics

↓
Semiconductor Physics: Energy bands in intrinsic and extrinsic silicon, carrier transport (diffusion, drift), generation and recombination.
Junctions & Transistors: PN junction diode, Zener diode, BJT, MOS capacitor, MOSFET (I-V characteristics, threshold voltage).
Optoelectronics: Photodiode, solar cell, LED.
AC

Analog Circuits

Diode circuits, small-signal models, Op-Amp circuits, active filters, and oscillators

↓
Diode & Transistor Circuits: Clipping, clamping, rectifiers. Small-signal equivalent circuits of BJTs and MOSFETs.
Amplifiers: Single-stage amplifiers, biasing, frequency response, feedback amplifiers.
Op-Amps: Ideal and practical Op-Amps, amplifiers, integrators, differentiators, active filters, oscillators (Wien-bridge, RC phase-shift, Hartley/Colpitts).
DC

Digital Circuits

Boolean algebra, logic gates, combinational/sequential logic, ADC/DAC, and memories

↓
Combinational Circuits: Boolean algebra, logic minimization (K-Maps), multiplexers, decoders, adders.
Sequential Circuits: Flip-flops, registers, counters, finite state machines (FSM).
Data Converters & Memory: ADC (successive approximation, flash), DAC (R-2R ladder), ROM, RAM, FPGA architectures.
CS

Control Systems

Transfer functions, feedback, stability criteria, and frequency response analysis

↓
System Modeling: Signal flow graphs, block diagrams, transfer functions of LTI systems.
Stability: Routh-Hurwitz criterion, Root Locus technique.
Frequency Domain: Bode plots, Nyquist plots, polar plots, lead-lag compensator designs.
CO

Communications

Random processes, analog modulations, digital modulations, and information theory

↓
Random Processes: Autocorrelation, power spectral density, white noise through LTI filters.
Analog Modulation: Amplitude modulation (AM, DSB-SC, SSB, VSB), frequency modulation (FM), phase modulation (PM).
Digital Modulation: PCM, DPCM, DM. Digital passband keying (ASK, FSK, PSK, QAM), matched filter receiver, bit error rate (BER) calculations.
Information Theory: Entropy, mutual information, Shannon's channel capacity.
EM

Electromagnetics (EMT)

Maxwell equations, boundary conditions, wave propagation, transmission lines, and antennas

↓
Maxwell's Equations: Differential and integral forms, boundary conditions, wave equation, Poynting vector.
Wave Propagation: Plane waves in lossy and lossless media, reflection, refraction, polarization.
Transmission Lines: Characteristic impedance, reflection coefficient, standing wave ratio, Smith Chart, impedance matching.
Waveguides & Antennas: TE and TM modes in rectangular waveguides. Dipole antennas, radiation pattern, gain, and directivity.
